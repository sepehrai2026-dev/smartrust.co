Why estate planning matters

Protect your family's future

Without a proper estate plan, your assets go through probate — a public, expensive, and slow court process. A well-structured trust can avoid probate, reduce estate taxes, protect assets from creditors, and ensure your wishes are carried out exactly as intended.

It's not just for the wealthy

Many people think estate planning is only for millionaires. In reality, anyone with a home, retirement accounts, or children should have at least a basic estate plan. A simple revocable living trust can cost less than a single month's mortgage payment.

Types of trusts

Revocable living trusts are the most common — you maintain control and can change them anytime. Irrevocable trusts offer stronger asset protection and tax benefits but can't be easily modified. Special needs trusts protect benefits eligibility. Charitable trusts reduce taxes while supporting causes you care about.
